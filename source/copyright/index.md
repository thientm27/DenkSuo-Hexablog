# Copyright Agreement

To maintain article quality, embrace the spirit of open sharing on the internet, and ensure stable page traffic, **all original articles on this site** are licensed under the stricter [Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives 4.0 International (CC BY-NC-ND 4.0)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-nd/4.0/deed.en) license. This article aims to clearly explain the licensing standards and requirements of this site to help you reasonably use the content.

> This site contains no ads or commercial activity.  
> Violating this license harms both the author’s enthusiasm and the overall copyright environment.  
> We strongly encourage compliance, which will not negatively impact your goals and helps foster a better creative space for everyone.

## Which articles are covered by this license?

All original content is marked at the top of each article and at the end in the copyright section.

- **Non-commercial reproduction of original content** must be complete and include a **clearly visible link** to the original (with text like `full URL` or `read original article`).
- If used as a reference, you may quote **partial content**, but must still credit the source.

## What are you allowed to do?

As long as you comply with the license terms, you are free to share the article content — by copying and distributing it in any medium or format, **without notifying the author**.

## What license terms must you follow?

### Attribution

You **must credit the original source**, ideally at the beginning of the article (or in a clearly visible spot), and include a link to the original post (preferably a hyperlink for better user experience).

### Non-Commercial Use Only

- This content is free to all internet users.
- **Commercial use is prohibited**, including placing ads (e.g., Google Ads, Baidu Ads) or gating content behind paywalls, subscriptions, or app downloads.

### No Derivative Works

- **Sharing full content (no modification)** is allowed, but you must include a source link at the beginning or in a clear location.
- **Sharing modified or excerpted content** (derivatives) is not allowed. If you have suggestions for improvements or want to share a derivative version, please comment under the original post to request permission.
- **Using content as reference** is allowed; you can excerpt relevant portions, but must credit the source.

### What content is protected by copyright?

Includes but is not limited to:

- Article cover images
- Article titles and body text
- Site image assets (excluding theme’s built-in assets)

### Exceptions

- **Friendly Linked Blogs**: Blogs listed in our friend links are allowed to share and distribute derivative works, but must still credit the original.
- **Requesting Permission**: In the spirit of openness, you can request permission in the comments section of the article to share derivative works. Be sure to include your website URL.
